# It's Just Luck

An interactive essay on the science of serendipity — why good luck is a skill, not a gift.

Live: **https://jenslaufer.com/serendipity/**

## What it is

A single-file `index.html` essay with three interactive Canvas "games", after Christian Busch's *The Serendipity Mindset* (2020). It walks the verified Busch process — **trigger → bisociation → tenacity** — and ends on the convex bridge to optionality.

1. **The trigger** — serendipity scales with surface area. Exposure × alertness sets how many unexpected events cross your path. Turn exposure to zero and a sharp mind still sees nothing.
2. **Bisociation** — an unexpected find is worthless until it meets a question you're already carrying. The prepared mind (Pasteur) connects the dots; a wandering one walks past them.
3. **Tenacity** — each connected lead is a small, droppable bet with a fat tail. Chase many → floor the loss, keep the upside (convex). Wait for one big break → a coin flip.

## Sources & honesty

- The **trigger → bisociation → tenacity** process and "smart luck" vs "blind luck" are Christian Busch's.
- "Bisociation" is Arthur Koestler's (*The Act of Creation*, 1964).
- Walpole's 1754 coinage and Pasteur's 1854 "prepared mind" are quoted at their verified wording.
- The convex / optionality bridge is the author's own synthesis, not a Busch claim.
- The games are deliberately simple toy models — directionally honest, not literal.
