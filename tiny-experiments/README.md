# It's Just an Experiment

An interactive, single-file web essay on **tiny experiments, growth loops, and optionality** — after Anne-Laure Le Cunff's *Tiny Experiments: How to Live Freely in a Goal-Obsessed World* (2025).

A sibling to [It's Just Math](https://jenslaufer.com/convex/): the same convexity argument, applied to a life instead of a portfolio.

**Live:** https://jenslaufer.com/tiny-experiments/

Three interactive games:

1. **Ladders & loops** — why a *drifting* world (you can't predict what you'll want) rewards the loop that re-aims every step over the ladder that commits to one prediction. Dramatizes "we don't go in circles; we grow in circles."
2. **The PACT** — why grading yourself on the **output** you control (Purposeful, Actionable, Continuous, Trackable) instead of the **outcome** the world controls makes a goal almost impossible to fail. Plus the Triple Check (Head / Heart / Hand → *aligned aliveness*).
3. **Optionality** — why many small, reversible, capped-downside experiments compound into a convex payoff that one big plan can't match — the bridge Le Cunff herself draws to Taleb in *The optionality fallacy*.

## Sources

- Anne-Laure Le Cunff, *Tiny Experiments* (Penguin/Portfolio, 2025).
- Ness Labs: ["SMART goals are not so smart: make a PACT instead"](https://nesslabs.com/smart-goals-pact) and ["The optionality fallacy"](https://nesslabs.com/optionality-fallacy).
- The Triple Check is after Hugo M. Kehr's three-component model of motivation.
- Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Antifragile* (2012), for the convexity framing.

The interactive games are deliberately simple toy models — directionally honest, not empirical claims.
